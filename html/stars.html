    Paint the Stars
    Fall 2020, 8 weeks

    The Bloomberg Philanthropies Asphalt Art Initiative aims to help cities around the United States improve street safety by revitalizing public spaces through art and community engagement. In 2020-21, Pittsburgh was chosen as one of 16 cities to receive a grant for this initiative.

    Paint the Stars is our proposal to bring the night sky down to Pittsburgh, to enjoy it with their fellow neighbors, friends, and family. Our goal is to bring the community closer together through constellations we all can share, cherish, and enjoy. We want to engage the community into helping us paint the pavements on Painting Day and bring our asphalt art to life.

    Asphalt Art –

    We took constellations seen in the Pittsburgh night sky, and have translated it as authentically as we could onto the roads. From the center of the intersection, the constellations become smaller and easier to see for everyday pedestrians. This was done to encourage people to stay along the crosswalk, while still being able to safely interact with the artwork.

    Visual System —

    The muted, galaxy colors juxtapose with the bright ones found in our constellation dots. For our wordmark, we played around with placement of the letterforms of the typeface, Stella (created by AnnMarie Weidenbenner), to mimic freeform constellations.

    01 Promotional Assets –

    In order to engage the community to sign up in painting the pavements, we designed posters focusing on the balance of hierarchy, color, and type, while staying true to our visual system.


    Promotional Posters

    Once they sign up, participants will receive a package in the mail with several items.


    Mailer Package Box

    It will contain a calendar marked with important dates leading up to, during, and after the painting event.


    Calendar of Events

    There will also be postcards that can be shared and colored in with friends and family by using the branded crayons which also come in the package.


    Postcards + Crayons

    02 Day of the Event —

    Come the day of, participants will be greeted with our welcome banner.


    Welcome Banner

    After their arrival, they will be split into groups that represent where they are assigned to paint to allow for more organization. Participants will be guided to sign up and be given a colored pin that represents their assigned sections.


    Sign Up Sheets + Pins

    In order to avoid confusion, there will be wayfinding signage throughout the intersection that will guide participants to the correct area, as well as a banner.


    Wayfinding Signage

    03 Permanent Structures —

    After the event, there will be permanent designs that will encourage the community to continue interacting with the asphalt art and one another, even after the asphalt is painted.

    There will be a kiosk that shows people their astrology sign. For example, pressing the Capricorn button will correspond to that sign lighting up on the kiosk. Then, participants are encouraged to go find their sign somewhere along the crosswalks, the color of their sign being the same on both the kiosk and crosswalk.


    Astrology Kiosk

    We will also have stools spread throughout the intersection sidewalks. To relax, spend time with friends, or play games around, people are encouraged to interact, move, and have fun with these stools. We chose a cylindrical shape so that when seen from a birds eye view, they mimic our constellation asphalt design.


    Constellation Stools

    Lastly, located by the Baum Grove Parklet, we have a large calendar that will be updated with events that the community will put on. From community picnics in the park, to stargazing nights, the entire community is encouraged to update the calendar with events so to continue building strong relationships with one another.


    Communal Calendar of Events
